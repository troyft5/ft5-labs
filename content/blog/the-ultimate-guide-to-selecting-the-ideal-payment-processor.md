Choosing a payment processor feels simple until you're locked into a multi-year contract, facing unexplained holds on your funds, or reading a monthly statement you can't decipher. Here's a practical framework for making the right call.

## Step 1: Understand Your Business Profile

Before comparing processors, get clear on your own numbers:

- **Average ticket size.** A $15 coffee shop and a $2,500 B2B invoice have completely different optimal pricing models.
- **Monthly volume.** High-volume merchants have negotiating power. Know your numbers.
- **Card mix.** Consumer debit, consumer credit, corporate cards, and rewards cards all have different interchange rates. Your effective rate depends heavily on what cards your customers use.
- **Transaction type.** Card-present (in-person) typically costs less than card-not-present (online, keyed-in).

## Step 2: Know the Processor Types

**Traditional merchant account providers** (First Data, TSYS, Heartland, Worldpay) offer direct relationships, customized pricing, and the ability to negotiate. Best for established businesses with consistent volume.

**Aggregators** (Stripe, Square, PayPal) bundle merchants together under a single master account. Easy to start, but rates are non-negotiable, and account stability decreases as volume grows.

**Payment facilitators** are hybrid models that combine aggregator simplicity with some direct merchant account benefits. Common in SaaS verticals.

## Step 3: Decode the Contract

Before signing anything, clarify:

- **Contract term.** Month-to-month vs. multi-year? Early termination fees?
- **Rate lock.** Are your rates guaranteed, or can the processor adjust them annually?
- **Funding speed.** Next-day funding, or does your cash sit for 2–3 days?
- **Reserve requirements.** Some processors hold a percentage of volume in reserve, particularly for new merchants.
- **PCI compliance.** Is there an annual PCI fee? Who handles your SAQ?

## Step 4: Compare Apples to Apples

The biggest mistake merchants make is comparing headline rates. "2.6% + $0.10" isn't comparable to "interchange-plus 0.30% + $0.10" unless you know your interchange baseline.

Request:
- Interchange-plus pricing quotes (not tiered)
- Month-to-month terms
- Your estimated effective rate based on your actual card mix
