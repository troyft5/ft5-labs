Payment processing fees are the charges businesses incur when accepting payments through credit cards, debit cards, and other electronic methods. They directly affect your bottom line — and most merchants are overpaying without realizing it.

## The Three-Layer Fee Structure

Every card transaction runs through three layers of cost:

**1. Interchange Fees**
Set by Visa and Mastercard, paid to the card-issuing bank. These vary by card type (consumer vs. corporate, debit vs. credit, rewards vs. standard) and transaction type (card-present vs. card-not-present). Interchange ranges from 0.05% to over 3% depending on these variables.

**2. Assessment Fees**
Charged by the card networks themselves (Visa, Mastercard, Discover, Amex). These are smaller — typically 0.13% to 0.15% — but unavoidable.

**3. Processor Markup**
This is where most of the hidden cost lives. Your processor adds a markup on top of interchange and assessments. This can be structured as:
- **Interchange-Plus:** A fixed markup on top of actual interchange. Most transparent.
- **Tiered:** Transactions bucketed into "qualified," "mid-qualified," and "non-qualified." Profitable for processors, confusing for merchants.
- **Flat-Rate:** Simple and predictable, but rarely the cheapest option at volume.

## How Fees Hit Your Actual Margin

Consider a restaurant doing $50,000/month in card volume on a 2.9% flat rate. That's $1,450/month — $17,400/year — in processing fees. If the same restaurant qualified for interchange-plus at an effective rate of 1.8%, they'd pay $900/month and save $6,600 annually. That's a family vacation. That's new equipment. That's profit.

## Common Hidden Fees to Watch For

- **PCI Non-Compliance Fee:** $20–$100/month if you haven't completed your annual SAQ
- **Statement Fee:** $5–$15/month for a PDF you can generate yourself
- **Batch Processing Fee:** Charged per daily settlement batch
- **IRS Reporting Fee:** Passed through to merchants despite being regulatory overhead for processors
- **Early Termination Fee:** Can be $300–$500+ if you exit a long-term contract

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Request an interchange-plus restatement.** Ask your current processor to show you what you paid in interchange, assessments, and markup separately.
2. **Get your effective rate.** Divide total processing fees by total volume. If it's above 2.2%, you're almost certainly overpaying.
3. **Get competitive quotes.** Most merchants never shop processors. A single audit from FinTech 5 returns quotes from 10+ providers in 24 hours.

Processing fees aren't fixed costs. They're negotiable — if you know what you're looking for.
